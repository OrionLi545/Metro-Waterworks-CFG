As steam enters the chambers, it expands and pushes down on the enclosed pistons to create vertical motion in the piston rods below. The Leavitt engine is a triple expansion engine, meaning the power of the steam is used three times across three separate steam chambers to power three separate pumps. The power of the steam is strongest in the first chamber. As it begins to cool off and lose power, in other words, as the force being exerted over the pistons lessens, the area of each successive steam chamber and piston needs to be increased to insure an equal output of pressure on all three vertical piston rods (Pressure = Force/Area). These steam chambers are jacketed to prevent condensation and minimize loss of heat. In this case, they are clad in walnut wood.
